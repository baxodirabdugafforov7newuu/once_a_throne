Chapter 1

12-13th centuries

Phase 1

Rise of the Khwarazmian Empire –
Expansion under
Ala ad-Din Muhammad.

Phase 2

Mongol Invasion (1219–1221) –
Genghis Khan’s attack and
destruction of major cities.

Phase 3

Jaloliddin Manguberdi’s Leadership –
His escape and resistance
against the Mongols.

Phase 4

Battle of the Indus (1221) –
His last stand against the Mongols,
forced to flee.

Phase 5

Guerrilla Warfare & Exile –
His attempts to regain power across
Persia and the Middle East.

Phase 6

Death and Legacy –
His assassination and the
end of Khwarazmian resistance.

Chapter 2

14-15 centuries

Phase 1

Timur seizes power in Samarkand,
laying the foundation for his empire.

Phase 2

Timur armies sweep through Persia,
India, and Russia,
crushing empires in his path.

Phase 3

Amir Timur defeats the
Ottoman Sultan Bayezid,
cementing his dominance
in the region.

Phase 4

Timur dies while marching to China,
leaving behind an empire on
the verge of chaos.

Phase 5

Shah Rukh maintains stability
through diplomacy and culture,
making Herat a center of art.

Phase 6

Civil war weakens the empire,
and the Uzbeks, led by Shaybanids,
take over the land.

Chapter 3

15-17 centuries

Phase 1

Shaybanids take Samarkand,
ending Timurid rule.
Babur flees to India.

Phase 2

Shaybani conquers Bukhara and Herat,
defeating the Timurids.
Killed by the Safavids.

Phase 3

Persians invade,
seeking control of Samarkand.
Uzbeks fight back.

Phase 4

Abdullah Khan II expands the empire.
Bukhara becomes the capital.

Phase 5

Bukhara, Khiva, and Kokand emerge.
Rivalries weaken the Uzbeks.

Phase 6

Rahim Khan strengthens Khiva’s rule.
Kokand gains power.

Chapter 4

18-19th centuries

Phase 1

Rahim Khan I expands Khiva’s power,
but Russian and Persian threats
loom over the region.

Phase 2

Kokand rises under Muhammad Ali Khan,
dominating Fergana before
internal strife weakens it.

Phase 3

Bukhara and Khiva struggle
for dominance while
Russian influence steadily increases.

Phase 4

Russia invades Khiva in 1873,
forcing the khan to surrender and
accept Russian rule.

Phase 5

Kokand falls to Russia in 1876,
ending its independence as it
becomes part of Turkestan.

Phase 6

By the 1920s, Khiva and Bukhara are
overthrown by the Soviets, marking
the end of the khanates.

Chapter 5

20th century

Phase 1

In 1924, the Uzbek SSR is formed,
redrawing borders and enforcing
Soviet rule through collectivization.

Phase 2

Stalin’s purges in the 1930s
target Uzbek intellectuals and leaders.
Thousands are executed or
sent to the Gulag.

Phase 3

World War II brings forced
deportations. Entire ethnic groups are
exiled to Uzbekistan
by Stalin’s orders.

Phase 4

The cotton industry expands
under Soviet plans, but
corruption and forced
labor worsen conditions.

Phase 5

The Aral Sea shrinks due
to Soviet irrigation projects,
creating an environmental disaster.

Phase 6

Uzbekistan declares independence
in 1991 as the Soviet Union collapses,
ending decades of control.

Chapter 6

1991 -

Phase 1

In the 1990s, Uzbekistan stabilizes
under Islam Karimov, focusing on
state-building and security.

Phase 2

Economic challenges shape the 2000s,
with efforts to strengthen industry
and reduce reliance on imports.

Phase 3

In 2005, unrest in Andijan
leads to a government response,
drawing international attention.

Phase 4

Uzbekistan shifts its foreign policy,
balancing relations between
Russia, China, and the West.

Phase 5

After 2016, Shavkat Mirziyoyev
introduces reforms, improving trade,
governance, and regional ties.

Phase 6

Ongoing modernization in the 2020s
sees economic growth, infrastructure
expansion, and legal reforms.

Famous Landmarks of Uzbekistan

Registan Square

The heart of Samarkand, featuring grand madrasahs.

Itchan Kala

A well-preserved walled city in Khiva with stunning architecture.

Bukhara Ark Fortress

A massive fortress that once housed Bukhara's rulers.

Registan Square

Registan Square in Samarkand was the center of the Timurid Empire. It features three madrasahs built between the 15th and 17th centuries, known for their intricate blue tilework and grand architecture.

Itchan Kala

Itchan Kala is the inner city of Khiva, surrounded by high walls. It served as an important stop along the Silk Road and features well-preserved Islamic architecture.

Bukhara Ark Fortress

The Ark Fortress in Bukhara was a massive stronghold used by rulers for centuries. It functioned as a royal court and military base.
